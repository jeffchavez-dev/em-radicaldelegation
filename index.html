Leverage and Engagement Managers

Chapter 2 — The force multiplier mindset

Summary

Working with an EA is not about saving 2–3 hours a day — it's about becoming a "legendary leader." An EM uses their EA to eliminate cognitive load across all areas: inbox, scheduling, team management, and relationship-building. The EA becomes a force multiplier, letting the EM operate at their highest-leverage activities only. The goal isn't productivity — it's transformation. The EM becomes someone others trust, believe in, follow, and support.

Core concepts

Massive LeverageEA's value is transformation of how you lead, not just efficiency
Cognitive Load ReductionRemove low-value responsibilities from your mental bandwidth
Legendary Leader FrameSomeone others trust, follow, and support
Extreme DelegationOffload anything where your value-add is minimal
Always In FlowSpend max time on high-value, energizing work
AdaptabilityEA keeps you responsive during unexpected disruptions

EA action plan

1
Calendar & time audit

EA reviews the past 2 weeks of EM's calendar. Identifies all tasks where EM's value-add is low (scheduling, logistics, admin, travel). Creates a "What EM is doing that EA should own" list.

Sample audit categories

Low-leverage tasks to hand off immediately

• Scheduling and rescheduling meetings • Travel booking and logistics • Responding to calendar invites • Sending meeting confirmations and reminders • Tracking action items from meetings • Following up on unanswered emails • Sending thank-you or acknowledgment emails • Researching contacts before meetings • Managing recurring admin tasks (expense reports, etc.)
2
Immediate handoff

EA takes over scheduling, travel, and calendar management now. EM stops responding to scheduling emails — EA handles all of it. EA sets up a shared task system for quick delegation via voice note or message.

3
Weekly delegation review

Standing 30-min weekly sync. Agenda: what got done, what's pending, what new tasks can EA absorb this week. Goal: absorb 1–2 new responsibility categories per month.

4
Define "legendary leader" goals

EM writes 3 goals for how they want to show up differently (e.g., fully present in 1:1s, 5 hrs/week on strategy, 24-hr response to every team member). EA's job is to protect EM's time so those goals are achievable.

5
Remove low-value commitments

EA audits recurring meetings EM attends but doesn't drive. Flags any that can be declined, delegated, or shortened. EM approves; EA handles calendar changes and communications.

eDrafting

Chapter 3 — Never write another follow-up email again

Summary

Immediately after any meeting, the EM dictates a 40-second voice note on their phone. The EA uses a 7-part template to turn it into a polished, personalized follow-up email — sent within hours. The dictation uses phone speech-to-text + ChatGPT. Over time, the EA learns your voice and tone. Every person you meet gets a high-quality personal follow-up, making you stand out in any professional network.

Core concepts

40-Second Follow-UpShort voice dictation captured immediately after any meeting
7-Part Email AnatomyHeader → Greeting → Positive Impact → Common Ground → Key Takeaways → Future-Pace → Sign-Off
Priority Dictation LabelFlags Priority Level, Timestamp, Future Pacing, Sign-Off needs
Dictation SystemPhone speech-to-text → EA inbox → EA drafts via template + ChatGPT
Specificity is KingMore specific positive impact & common ground = better email
AI DraftingChatGPT takes the rough dictation and writes the structured email

EA action plan

1
Set up the dictation system

EA creates a dedicated email or Slack channel for all dictations. EM installs a voice-to-text app (phone native or Otter.ai). EA gets ChatGPT access for drafting.

2
Create the 7-part follow-up email template

EA builds and maintains a shared Google Doc using the anatomy below. Every follow-up email must hit all 7 parts.

The 7-part email anatomy with examples

Part I — Header (internal — for EA only, not in the email)

Who: [Recipient name + phonetic spelling if needed] Priority: High / Normal Send by: [Day + time] Future pace: [Y/N — does EM want to schedule the next meeting?] Sign-off word: "Cheers" / "Best" / "Thanks" — signals end of dictation to EA

Part II — Greeting

"Hey Sachin, it was great to connect. I really enjoyed getting to know you and learning a little more about what you're doing at Catalyst."

Part III — Positive Impact (be specific)

"I don't think I've ever met someone with such a grounded view of the future of AI. Your vision for automated vehicles is truly fascinating." No matter how "bad" a meeting is, there's almost always something positive you can comment on. Even if you disagreed, try: "I really appreciate the way you and I can have an open discussion about these things, even when we disagree. I just want you to know that..."

Part IV — Common Ground

"Beyond that, I'm thrilled to meet another craft beer enthusiast. It's not often I meet someone who seems to have explored more of the Bay's microbreweries than I have. I'm really looking forward to checking out Drake's Brewery in San Leandro. I'll definitely try the Drakonic stout."

Part V — Key Takeaways

"Besides getting to know you better, here's the key next steps as I remember them from our meeting: - I offered to send you the link to Paul Graham's maker's vs manager's time article. Becca, do you think you could find and include that down and include the link in the email? - He offered to introduce me to his co-founder Eric if he's open to it. - If you think I left out any important aspects of the conversation, please let me know."

Part VI — Future-Pace

"Otherwise, I've CC'd Becca to help us find a time to connect sometime next month."

Part VII — Sign-Off

"Cheers, Blake" Note: Always use the same sign-off as a signal to your EA that the email is over.
3
Set up the priority label system

In each dictation's subject line or tag: Priority (High/Normal), Timestamp (when to send), Future Pace (schedule next meeting? Y/N), Sign-Off signals end of dictation.

Sample priority dictation label format

Priority label structure

Priority Level: [High / Normal] — "High" means your EA should try to send the email before the end of the day. — "Normal" means within 24 hours is fine. Day/Timestamp: [e.g., "Send today by 5pm" or "Send tomorrow morning"] Future Pacing: [Y / N] — "Y" means include a line offering to schedule a follow-up meeting. — "N" means no scheduling needed. Sign-Off: [Your standard closing, e.g., "Cheers, Jeff"] — The moment your EA hears your sign-off word, they know the dictation is complete.
4
EM commits to the habit — the 40-second dictation

After every meaningful meeting, dictate immediately — in the car, on a walk, at the elevator. Cover: who you met, what stood out, what was discussed, any follow-up actions. Send to EA; EA handles the rest.

Calibrate the first 5 drafts together

EA drafts first 5 emails; EM reviews each. Give specific feedback on tone, warmth, and length. After 5 calibrated drafts, EA sends autonomously.

Scale to every meeting

Goal: every calendar meeting gets a follow-up drafted by EA. EA tracks which meetings have been debriefed and flags any missed. EM's only job: dictate within 30 minutes of the meeting ending.

Meeting Debriefs

Chapter 4 — How to be the most trustworthy person in anyone's network

Summary

After every meaningful meeting, the EM dictates a structured debrief using the APPA format — Action Items, Professional Challenges, Personal Interests & Passions, About/Facts. The EA converts this into a rich contact profile in a CRM, follows up on action items, and generates a Meeting Prebrief before every future meeting. The EM becomes the most prepared, trustworthy, and memorable person in any room — following through on 100% of commitments and remembering details others forget.

Core concepts

APPA FormatAction Items · Professional Challenges · Personal Interests & Passions · About/Facts
Meeting DebriefStructured dictation immediately after a meeting; stored in CRM
Meeting PrebriefEA-generated briefing sent to EM before every future meeting
Loop LeverageEA tracks every commitment until it's confirmed closed
Slim CommitmentsLow-effort actions (send article, make intro) EA can execute independently
True CommitmentsHigher-stakes promises requiring EM's direct involvement

EA action plan

1
Choose and set up a CRM

EA recommends a CRM (Notion, Salesforce, HubSpot, Clay). Creates an APPA contact template: Name, Role, Company, Action Items, Professional Challenges, Personal Interests & Passions, About/Facts. Imports existing contacts from EM's email/calendar.

2
Create the Meeting Debrief dictation template

EA creates a 1-page cheat sheet for EM to use on the go. Every debrief follows the APPA structure.

Sample debrief dictation script (APPA format)

What EM says into their phone immediately after the meeting

"Hey Becca, this is a meeting debrief for [Full Name — spell it out]. That's [spell phonetically]. This is a [Priority Level] meeting debrief. Action Items: — [List every commitment made by you or them] Professional Challenges: — [Career goals, frustrations, things they're working through professionally] Personal Interests and Passions: — [Hobbies, family, personal projects, things they're excited about outside work] About/Facts: — [Anything miscellaneous that makes them unique or memorable — health, family news, upcoming life events, fun facts] If I missed or misunderstood anything, please let me know. Otherwise, please add these to their contact record. Cheers, [Your Name]"
Tip: The "About/Facts" section is the most powerful long-term. These are the details that, when you bring them up at the next meeting, make people feel truly seen. "How's your daughter's marathon training going?" — nobody expects that.
3
EM starts debriefing every meeting

Dictate within 10 minutes of every meaningful meeting. EA updates the CRM contact record and sends an acknowledgment confirming the record was saved.

Real example: Sharon Murray Meeting Debrief (from the book)

Email Becca sent back to Blake after receiving the debrief dictation

Subject: Meeting Debrief! Sharon Murray
Hi Blake, This is the meeting debrief information I extracted for your meeting with Sharon Murray: Action Items: — You will share the Mindfulness Daily at Work program with Sharon. — Sharon will introduce you to her Bejoy co-founder Riley Stone. — You will ask Robert Ovens if he'd be open to an introduction to Sharon. Professional Challenges: — Sharon is considering taking an entrepreneur-in-residence position at Sequoia Capital but isn't sure if she wants to take her career in that direction. Personal Interests and Passions: — Sharon has found a new passion for marathons and will be completing her first at the Half Moon Bay Marathon on February 13th, 2021. She started training after seeing her daughter's drive to get back on the soccer field. — Sharon has recently started trying to adopt a mindfulness practice. About/Facts: — Her daughter Evelyn began a new asthma treatment program at Stanford University and has had very positive results so far. — Evelyn has returned to playing soccer again, and her team's last game of the season is January 18th, 2021. If you feel I missed or misunderstood anything, please let me know. Otherwise, I've added these to Sharon's contact record. Thanks, Becca
4
EA extracts and tracks action items

Each debrief → EA categorizes: Handled by EM (creates task + follows up in 48 hrs), Handled by EA (Slim Commitment, executes immediately), or Loop Leverage (recurring reminder until closed). EA sends a weekly "open commitments" digest.

Action item categories explained

How EA classifies every action item

Handled by You (EM): — Tasks that only EM can do (e.g., dictating a blog post, making a strategic decision). — EA creates a task in the task manager and checks in if not done within 48 hrs. Handled by Your EA: — Tasks EA can complete independently (e.g., finding and sending a blog post, drafting an intro email). — EA executes immediately upon receiving the debrief. Loop Leverage: — Commitments made to someone else (e.g., "I'll introduce you to Daniel"). — EA creates a recurring reminder and tracks status until confirmed closed. — If the other party hasn't followed through either, EA proactively follows up on your behalf.
5
Set up Meeting Prebriefs

EA sets a calendar trigger: any meeting with a known contact gets a Prebrief 24 hrs before. EM reads it on the way to the meeting — walks in fully prepared.

Full Meeting Prebrief format (from the book)

Prebrief email subject line

Subject: Meeting Prebrief | [Name] | [Company]

Part I — The Header

Name: [Link to LinkedIn profile for quick bio] Role: [Their title] Company: [Company name — linked to website] Meeting Location: [Address + directions, or Zoom link] Date/Time: [Day + time] For in-person calls: include the other party's phone number + who calls whom e.g., "Blake to call Sandra at 3pm" or "Sandra to call Blake at 3pm" Proposed Icebreaker Statement: A short, prepared conversation starter based on APPA info. e.g., "I've heard you've been in Mill Valley long enough to be called a local now, so Ryan's starting a local fund — have you tried calling it Ball Valley Colorado?"

Part II — APPA sections (pulled from CRM)

Action Items (pending): — List any outstanding commitments from either party Professional Challenges: — Career goals, frustrations, ambitions they've shared Personal Interests & Passions: — Hobbies, family, personal projects — things to ask about About/Facts: — The "miscellaneous" category — health, upcoming life events, things that make them unique

Part III — Additional context & next steps

APPA Misc. Info: [Anything EA wants to flag — e.g., "Can you ask them about their product launch?"] Next Steps: [What EM should try to accomplish in this meeting] Follow-up Reminder: [e.g., "Becca, could you make sure to schedule a follow-up if they commit to an intro?"]
The Meeting Prebrief is what makes every meeting feel like a reunion. It takes minutes for your EA to prepare, but the effect is that you walk in knowing what matters to this person — and you never waste the first 5 minutes "remembering" where you left off.
6
Monthly CRM review

EM and EA review the CRM together. Flag stale or incomplete contacts. EA reaches out to schedule re-engagement with lapsed relationships.

Inbox Shadowing

Chapter 5 — How to free yourself from your inbox once and for all

Summary

Inbox Shadowing means your EA proactively manages your entire inbox. It starts with Triaging (EA labels every email so you only touch what truly needs you), progresses to the EA drafting responses for review, and ultimately reaches full Shadowing where the EA handles most emails autonomously. The EM checks their inbox only 3 times a day. Beyond time savings, Inbox Shadowing enables proactive outreach at scale — thoughtful check-ins and relationship emails you'd never find time to write yourself.

Core concepts

Inbox TriagingEA labels every incoming email before EM sees it
Please HandleEA can respond fully without involving EM
FYIEM should be aware but no action required
UrgentRequires EM's direct attention or decision immediately
Everything Else FolderAll triaged emails archived here; EM's inbox stays near-zero
Proactive OutreachEA initiates meaningful touch-points using inbox + CRM context

EA action plan

Phase 1 — Setup (Week 1)

1
Grant inbox access

EM grants EA full inbox access (Google Workspace delegate or Outlook shared mailbox). Agree on a real-time escalation channel (Slack DM or text).

2
Create the three labels and folder structure

EA creates 3 labels in Gmail (or folders in Outlook): "Please Handle," "FYI," "Urgent" + an "Everything Else" archive folder. Optional: Gmail filters to auto-sort newsletters, notifications, and receipts directly to Everything Else.

What each label means — with examples

Please Handle — EA responds without EM

Use when: EA can draft and send a response without EM's direct input. Examples: — A scheduling request from a former colleague reaching out to catch up — An investor asking if you'd be interested in connecting with another portfolio CEO — A project status update asking you to confirm you're still aligned — Simple yes/no questions where context makes the answer obvious — Introduction requests EA can facilitate on your behalf

FYI — EM reads but doesn't need to respond

Use when: EM should know about this, but no action is required right now. Examples: — An email from a new team member introducing themselves — A service provider announcing an update or new feature — A newsletter or article that's genuinely relevant to EM's work — A team member sharing a project status that's already handled

Urgent — EM must respond directly

Use when: The email requires EM's direct opinion, decision, or action. Examples: — An email from your CEO asking for your take on a specific decision — A legal or compliance matter requiring EM's signature or acknowledgment — A sensitive personnel issue that only EM can address — A time-critical request that can't be delegated Note: "Urgent" should be rare. If your EA labels too many emails Urgent, revisit the definition together in your weekly feedback session.
3
Define "Please Handle" scope

EM and EA spend one session defining what EA can handle autonomously. Anything requiring EM's direct opinion → escalate to Urgent or ask EM.

Phase 2 — Inbox Triaging (Weeks 1–2)

4
EA triages only — no drafting yet

EA labels all incoming email. EM checks inbox 3x/day and corrects any misclassifications. No drafting yet — this phase builds classification accuracy only.

Sample "Please Handle" email EA receives and acts on

Incoming email EM receives

EA labels it "Please Handle" and drafts this reply autonomously

Subject: Re: New app store update is live!
Hey Ryan, This is great! Thanks so much for keeping me in the loop. I know a lot of work goes into updates like this, and I just wanted to say thanks for spearheading the project. Cheers, Blake
EA can handle: scheduling, acknowledgments, project status responses, introduction requests, simple yes/no answers. EA escalates: anything that needs EM's actual opinion, anything politically sensitive, anything involving direct reports' performance.
5
Weekly feedback session

Every week: EM reviews 5–10 recent labeling decisions with EA. Explains reasoning when EA got one wrong. Goal: 90%+ labeling accuracy within 2 weeks.

Phase 3 — Proactive Drafting (Weeks 3–4)

6
EA drafts basic responses

EA begins drafting replies for "Please Handle" emails, placed in Drafts with subject prefix "[DRAFT - Please review]." EM reviews, edits minimally, and sends — or approves for EA to send directly.

Sample "Please Handle" delegation dictation to EA

EM's voice note to EA when they need something specific handled

"Hey Becca, there's a scheduling update for Beth Luxmore. She's on my calendar for later today. But if I don't cancel it, I'm not going to have a chance to make those updates to our pitch deck. Could you reach out to let her know, and work with her to find another time next week? Please also send an email from me to Bella letting her know how sorry I am, and how much I appreciate her patience, and that I'm looking forward to connecting again soon. Thanks!"

Three-stage drafting progression

Stage 1 — Basic drafts (Weeks 3–4): EA drafts response → places in EM Drafts → EM reviews → EM sends Subject prefix: "[DRAFT - Please review]" Stage 2 — Autonomous drafts (Week 5+): EA drafts and sends "Please Handle" emails independently EA still flags anything unusual for EM awareness Stage 3 — Full Shadowing: EA owns the inbox proactively — not just reacting, but initiating outreach EM checks inbox 3x/day max, handles only Urgent items
7
EM checks inbox 3x/day only

Morning (8–9am), midday (12–1pm), end of day (5–6pm). All other time: inbox is EA's domain. Urgent Slack/text channel for anything that truly can't wait.

Phase 4 — Full Inbox Shadowing (Week 5+)

8
EA sends autonomously

EA sends "Please Handle" emails without EM's review. Complex emails go to Drafts for EM's 3x/day review. EM's inbox sessions drop from 30+ minutes to under 10 minutes.

9
Enable proactive outreach

EA uses inbox context + Meeting Debrief CRM to initiate meaningful touch-points on EM's behalf — without EM having to prompt it.

Monthly inbox health review

Is labeling still accurate? Are Urgent escalations appropriate — not too many (EA under-handling) or too few (things slipping)? Adjust scope of "Please Handle" as EA's context grows.
